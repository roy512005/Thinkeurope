IELTS Success: Prepare for Excellence

About International English Language Testing System (IELTS)

The IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a highly sought-after standardized test designed to evaluate an individual's proficiency in the English language. Attaining a strong IELTS score can provide a competitive advantage, positioning you at the forefront among applicants.

Reasons for Considering IELTS:

  1. IELTS scores are accepted by over 10,000 institutions worldwide.
  2. Annually, there are approximately 4 to 5 million IELTS test takers globally.
  3. IELTS is administered in 140 countries across the world.
  4. The test is conducted in over 1100 test centers globally.
  5. IELTS scores remain valid for a duration of two years.

The IELTS, a widely recognized English language proficiency test on a global scale, is a prerequisite for entry into certain international universities and employment opportunities abroad. A superior performance in the IELTS holds precedence over other candidates, as the test evaluates an individual's skills in reading, listening, writing, and speaking.

IELTS Exam Eligibility:

The IELTS exam is open to individuals aged 16 and above, serving as an English language proficiency test to demonstrate one's competency for admission to international universities. No specific educational qualifications are necessary for taking the IELTS. The exam is suitable for those:

  • Migrating to an English-speaking country
  • Enrolled in a university abroad
  • Seeking improved career prospects

There is no upper age limit; anyone above 16 years old can undertake the IELTS without a maximum age cap.

What is the IELTS Syllabus?

  • Listening
    • Four recorded monologues and conversations.
  • Reading
    • The reading section comprises long reading passages with tasks. Non-verbal material includes graphs, diagrams, and illustrations. 
  • Writing
    • By summarizing, explaining, or describing a table, graph, diagram, or chart, you have to write an essay with at least 250 words. 
  • Speaking
    • The speaking round includes a face-to-face interview to test a person's speaking skills. This session contains familiar topics. 

IELTS Exam Pattern

The IELTS test comprises 4 sections to test the English language ability of the participating person. Reading, listening, speaking. Writing skills will be tested during the exam.

Section Number of Questions Duration
Listening 4 questions 30 minutes
Reading 40 questions 60 minutes
Writing 2 questions 60 minutes
Speaking 3 questions 11 to 14 minutes

Where can I find the IELTS Mock Test?

Mock tests for IELTS serve as a valuable tool for evaluating your preparedness for the examination. Prior to the actual IELTS exam, candidates can utilize mock tests to gauge their proficiency in each section. The IELTS exam duration is 2 hours and 44 minutes, along with an additional 10 minutes for transfer time. Practicing with mock tests is essential for achieving optimal success in the IELTS exam.

IELTS Scoring and Bands

IELTS scores range from 0 to 9, including scores with .5 increments, such as 7.5 or 8.5. Each section—listening, writing, reading, and speaking—receives a band score from 0 to 9. The overall band score is computed as the average of all individual scores.

Calculation of IELTS Band Score:

  • The overall band score is determined by averaging the scores from all four sections. For instance, if you receive scores of Listening: 7, Reading: 7.5, Writing: 8, and Speaking: 7.5, your overall score would be 7.5 bands.
  • In cases where the score ends with .25, the band score is rounded up to the nearest .5. For example, a score of 7.25 is rounded up to 7.5.
  • If the score ends with .75, the band score is rounded to the nearest whole band. For instance, a score of 7.75 is rounded to 8.
  • If the score falls below .25 or .75, it is rounded down. For example, a score of 7.21 is rounded down to 7 bands.

IELTS Examination Overview

The IELTS test comprises two distinct types, each catering to specific purposes, and applicants are required to select the test that aligns with their immediate objectives.

1. IELTS Academic

Designed for undergraduate or postgraduate students seeking admission to foreign universities and those pursuing professional registration, the Academic test evaluates readiness for studying or training in an English language environment.

2. IELTS General Training Test

Tailored for professionals and migrants intending to establish permanent residence in an English-speaking country, this test assesses applicants' comfort in daily English language situations. A favorable score is particularly crucial for those aspiring to settle in countries like the UK, Canada, and Australia.

The IELTS Exam (PBT) is administered four times a month, and the online IELTS is conducted multiple times, facilitated by both the British Council and the IDP. Assistance in applying for an IELTS test is available through our Support Team.

Regarding IELTS

IELTS stands as a globally recognized standardized assessment of English language proficiency. The motivations for undertaking the IELTS test encompass various purposes, such as:

  1. Employment
  2. Pursuing higher education with an extended course duration (3 months or more)
  3. Enrolling in higher education short courses (3 months or less)
  4. Immigration
  5. Other educational pursuits
  6. Personal reasons
  7. Professional registration (excluding medical professions)
  8. Dentist registration
  9. Doctor registration
  10. Nurse registration (including CGFNS)

IELTS Types 

There are 2 types of IELTS tests available — IELTS Academic test and IELTS General Training test.

IELTS Academic

For study abroad

Suitable for those intending to study in an English-speaking environment or higher education. The IELTS Academic test is also accepted for professional registration purposes.

Vocabulary featured in IELTS Academic is that can be expected and familiar within an academic setting.


IELTS Academic Test Format [Total Duration: 2 hours 45 minutes]		  
Listening 30 minutes
Academic Reading 60 minutes
Academic Writing 60 minutes
Speaking 11 to 14 minutes

The Listening and Speaking tests are the same for IELTS General Training and IELTS Academic.

However, there are different Writing and Reading tests for the 2 types of IELTS.

IELTS General Training

For work overseas or migrate overseas

The IELTS General Training test is for those applying to study below degree level. The test might also be taken for employment training or work experience.

IELTS General Training test scores are required for migration to the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.

IELTS General Training test features skills in the English language that an individual will need in the workplace and other social environments.

IELTS General Training Test Format [Total Duration: 2 hours 45 minutes]  
Listening 30 minutes
General Training Reading 60 minutes
General Training Writing 60 minutes
Speaking 11 to 14 minutes

Which IELTS Test Type Is Suitable for Me?

IELTS Academic: If your intention is to relocate to an English-speaking country for educational purposes, especially at the university level, the IELTS Academic test is the appropriate choice. Additionally, IELTS Academic can be undertaken for professional registration purposes.

IELTS General Training: This test is applicable for individuals aiming to:

  • Apply for studies below the degree level,
  • Gain work experience or undergo employment training, and
  • Migrate to countries such as Canada, Australia, the UK, and New Zealand.

IELTS Validity

Your IELTS score remains valid for a duration of 2 years from the date of the examination. There is no restriction on the number of attempts; you can take the exam as many times as necessary.

IELTS Registration

To register for the IELTS exam, follow these steps:

  1. Visit the official IELTS website.
  2. Create a login account with a username and password.
  3. Provide all required information.
  4. Schedule an appointment for the IELTS exam date and time.
  5. Verify all details.
  6. Pay the IELTS registration fees.
  7. Click on the Register/Apply button.
  8. A confirmation will be sent to your registered mobile number.

IELTS Eligibility

Candidates meeting the following criteria are eligible for the IELTS test:

  • Minimum age of 16 years.
  • Ability to take IELTS regardless of grade 12 percentage.
  • No upper age limit or specific qualification requirement.
  • No limit on the number of attempts.

IELTS Requirements

To take the IELTS exam, you need to be at least 16 years old, and there is no maximum age limit. Your grade 12 percentages are not a factor in the IELTS exam. In terms of score requirements, achieving a minimum of 6.5 points or above is advisable for admission to top international universities.
